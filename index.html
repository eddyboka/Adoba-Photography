Annie Leibovitz

Annie Leibovitz is an American portrait photographer known for her intimate, often dramatic celebrity photographs. Born on October 2, 1949, in Waterbury, Connecticut, she began her career as a staff photographer for Rolling Stone magazine in 1970. Her distinctive style of capturing celebrities in unusual, artistic poses quickly gained attention. Leibovitz is particularly known for her work with high-profile figures such as John Lennon, who posed for her hours before his death in 1980, and her iconic portraits of Queen Elizabeth II, as well as her contributions to Vanity Fair and Vogue. Over the years, her work has helped shape the visual culture of modern celebrity and has been featured in numerous exhibitions around the world.

Devin Allen

Devin Allen is a renowned American photographer and activist, best known for his powerful images documenting the Black experience in the United States, particularly in the context of social justice movements. He gained significant attention for his photography of the 2015 Baltimore protests following the death of Freddie Gray, with his work capturing both the intensity and the resilience of the moment. Allen's photos have appeared in major publications like Time, The New York Times, and National Geographic. His work focuses on issues like race, identity, and the struggles faced by marginalized communities. Beyond photography, Allen is an advocate for social change, using his platform to amplify voices and bring attention to systemic issues.
